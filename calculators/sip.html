What is a SIP Calculator?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) calculator is a tool that helps you compute the future value of your monthly mutual fund investments. By leveraging the power of compound interest, SIP allows small monthly contributions to grow into a substantial corpus over time. Finzoop's SIP calculator gives you instant projections based on your expected rate of return.

How DOES the SIP Formula Work?

The mathematical formula used by mutual funds for calculating SIP returns is based on the future value of an annuity. The formula is:

M = P × ({[1 + i]^n – 1} / i) × (1 + i)

Where:

  • M = Maturity amount or Total Value
  • P = Monthly SIP amount
  • i = Monthly interest rate (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)
  • n = Number of months (Years × 12)

Benefits of SIP Investing

  1. Rupee Cost Averaging: You buy more units when the market is down and fewer units when it is up, lowering your average cost per unit.
  2. Power of Compounding: Returns generated on your investment generate further returns. The longer you stay invested, the steeper the wealth creation curve.
  3. Discipline: Automatic monthly deductions ensure you save before you spend, embedding crucial financial discipline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are SIP returns guaranteed?

No. Mutual fund SIP returns are linked to the performance of the financial markets (equity or debt). However, historically, equity mutual funds have delivered inflation-beating returns over long periods (7+ years).

Can I pause or stop my SIP anytime?

Yes, mutual funds offer absolute flexibility. You can pause, stop, or modify your SIP amount at any time without any penalties, unless the fund is an ELSS (which has a 3-year lock-in per installment).

Is 12% a realistic return expectation?

For diversified equity mutual funds in India, a long-term CAGR of 10% to 12% is considered a realistic assumption based on historical data. However, shorter horizons may experience significant volatility.

Does SIP save taxes?

Only investments made in Equity Linked Savings Schemes (ELSS) via SIP qualify for tax deduction under Section 80C up to ₹1.5 Lakhs. Regular equity and debt SIPs do not offer 80C benefits.
