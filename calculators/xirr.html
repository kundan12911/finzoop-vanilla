XIRR Calculator

Measure returns for irregular investments and SIPs accurately.

Why use XIRR?

When you invest a single amount and withdraw it later, calculating the Annualised Return (CAGR) is easy. But what if you invest ₹5000 in January, withdraw ₹2000 in April, and invest ₹10000 in August?

Because your cash flows are happening at irregular intervals, standard CAGR fails. XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) assigns a specific weight to each cash flow based on the exact number of days it was invested.

How to calculate XIRR easily:

  1. Open Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets.
  2. In Column A, enter the exact Dates of your transactions.
  3. In Column B, enter the Amounts. Use negative values (-5000) for your investments, and positive values (12500) for withdrawals / current portfolio value.
  4. Use the formula: =XIRR(B1:B10, A1:A10)
  5. Format the result as a Percentage to view your actual return.
