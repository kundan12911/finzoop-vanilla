How to Choose the Right Credit Card

Choosing a credit card depends entirely on your spending habits. If you travel frequently by air, a travel card offering lounge access and airmiles makes sense even if it has an annual fee. If you spend mostly on groceries and utility bills, a cashback card offering straight 5% cashback will provide the best absolute returns.

  • Check your credit score: Premium cards usually require a CIBIL score of 750+.
  • Analyse your major spends: Don't pay for premium travel benefits if you rarely fly.
  • Look at the annual fee waiver: Most cards waive the fee if you spend above a certain threshold annually.
